        <rule name="agent_asked">If the agent directly asks about a budget category, it counts as collected regardless of the client's response. The agent asking is what matters.</rule>
        <rule name="customer_volunteered">If the customer provides budget information for a category without being asked, it counts as collected. The information was obtained even though the agent didn't prompt it.</rule>
        <rule name="broad_question">If the agent asks a broad financial question (e.g., "What are your monthly expenses?") and the client's response covers a budget category, that category counts as collected. The broad question must result in the client actually mentioning the category.</rule>
        <rule name="not_applicable_still_counts">The agent must ask about each required category even if it doesn't apply to the client. For example, asking "Any car payments?" and getting "No" still counts as collected - the agent verified the item.</rule>
        <rule name="credit_report_exclusion">Debts already visible on the credit report being discussed do NOT count as collecting the "other debts" category. The agent must ask about expenses and obligations beyond what appears on the credit report.</rule>
        <rule name="unclear_default">When the transcript is ambiguous about whether a budget category was discussed, default to marking it as skipped. Err on the side of flagging to ensure compliance.</rule>
        <rule name="required_vs_optional">Required fields (marked with required="true") are the ones that determine whether a violation occurred. Optional fields should still be tracked but do not factor into the violation threshold.</rule>
